Data Analysis: Patterns, Prediction and Causality

A graduate textbook for Business, Economics and Policy


Data Analysis: Patterns, Prediction and Causality is a textbook that covers the most important methods and tools data analysts need in the fields of business, economics and public policy. It provides integrated knowledge of methods traditionally scattered around econometrics, machine learning, and practical business statistics. It covers data wrangling, exploratory data analysis, regression analysis, predictive analytics by regression and tree-based methods, and causal analysis by doing experiments and using observational data. The textbook covers relatively few methods but helps students gain a lot of practice and a deep intuitive understanding of those methods. We put a lot of emphasis on the interpretation and visualization of results.

The textbook has 24 chapters sctructured in four parts:

I. Data Exploration
II. Patterns: regression analysis
III. Prediction
IV. Causality: the effects of interventions

We help students gain a working knowledge of the most important tools and metods through many applications. The textbook includes many case studies and additional data exercises. All are bsed on real-life data and illustrate how to manage analytical projects from scratch. We provide data and code as part to all case studies in R and Stata (Python codes are in the works).
